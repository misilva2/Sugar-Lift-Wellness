Our Story

Tiffany Silva was diagnosed with Type-1 diabetes and built a practice rooted in empathy, education, and empowerment. Sugar Lift Wellness began with a mission to bridge healthcare gaps in rural communities—bringing trusted guidance to families who need it most.

Our Support Staff: We've built a dedicated bilingual team to ensure every patient receives personalized care in their preferred language—English or Spanish—removing language barriers that often prevent effective diabetes education.

Accessible Services for Rural Communities

Breaking down barriers with telehealth, multilingual support, and coordinated care designed for underserved areas.

  • Telehealth Consultations

    Virtual one-on-one certified diabetes education sessions—no travel required. Our support staff provides consultations in English and Spanish for ongoing continuity of care.

  • Community Education Workshops

    Free bilingual group sessions led by our support staff, addressing diabetes prevention, self-management, and complications. Available in English and Spanish at rural community centers and clinics.

  • Care Coordination Support

    Bridging gaps between specialty care and primary care. We work with your existing providers to ensure continuous, comprehensive diabetes management.

  • Remote Monitoring Guidance

    CGM setup, interpretation, and troubleshooting support via telehealth to overcome limited local specialty access.

  • Community Health Worker Training

    Building local capacity by training community members to provide peer support and basic diabetes education in underserved areas.

  • Emergency & After-Hours Resources

    24/7 access to educational materials, decision support tools, and emergency protocols for when specialist care isn't immediately available.

Program Model

A two-phase approach that builds rural diabetes education capacity while delivering accessible, culturally responsive care.

  • Phase 1: Healthcare Workforce Development

    Structured diabetes education and skills training for individuals managing diabetes and medical support staff.

  • Training Focus Areas

    Foundational diabetes knowledge, medication and insulin safety, glucose monitoring technologies, patient education strategies, and preparation for diabetes educator certification pathways.

  • Flexible Delivery Formats

    In-person intensives, virtual sessions, and hybrid models to maximize rural accessibility.

  • Phase 2: Community Diabetes Education & Support

    Accessible diabetes self-management education and support (DSMES) for individuals and families through bilingual, culturally responsive classes, group education, and individualized coaching.

  • Barrier Reduction

    Services designed to reduce barriers to care, promote health literacy, and improve engagement across rural communities.

  • Long-Term Behavior Change

    Ongoing support and coaching that strengthen self-management habits and sustain positive outcomes over time.

Anticipated Outcomes

Measurable improvements we expect from expanding certified diabetes education across rural communities.

  • Stronger Self-Management

    Improved diabetes knowledge, self-management skills, and patient engagement through consistent education and coaching.

  • Fewer Preventable Emergencies

    Reduced preventable complications and lower emergency department utilization with proactive support and early intervention.

  • Expanded Rural Workforce Capacity

    Increased diabetes education capacity by training rural healthcare staff and community health workers.

  • Continuity Across Care Settings

    Strengthened continuity of care between acute, outpatient, and community settings to prevent gaps in follow-up.
